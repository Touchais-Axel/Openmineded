LEV, the loneliness that affects everyone

In 2019, rapper Nekfeu announced "Les Étoiles Vagabondes". The documentary is presented in the cinema and traces the conception of the new album of the artist during his travels around the world. In a single screening, the film is a huge success. I will explain why this work is a marvel of creativity.

A desire to flee

The beginning of the film takes place in Paris, the city in which Nekfeu spends most of his time. We feel that he is suffocating, that he needs to escape from the city of light.

The artist then takes all his friends on a journey to the destinations of Japan, Greece and the United States. Staying too long has made him lose all inspiration, he is a victim of the blank page.

Going to other countries is a radical but innovative solution. This journey will not only give him a change of scenery as a man, but also allow him to find new inspirations as an artist.

And inspirations lead to the most effective creativity. To seek new ideas throughout the world, it is finally the dream of any creator.

A universal film and album

Making a documentary film to accompany the release of an album is a fairly common idea today. Many artists have done it like Angèle, Bigflo and Oli, or Orelsan with his documentary "Montre jamais ça à personne" more recently.

But in 2019, Nekfeu was one of the first to do this, which makes it an innovative concept. The goal of an artist is to leave a mark through their work, so it's natural that other singers have followed suit.

But what makes the DNA of L.E.V. and distinguishes it from other documentaries is its diversity. The multitude of countries visited, of singers or more widely of different styles shown in the film, are an integral part of the album. This is what makes it unique, but also universal.

The rapper has shown creativity in the making of his project. He managed to mix sounds from all over the world to create songs that are now iconic.

On several occasions, we feel a real imprint of Japanese culture in the documentary. Like many, the rapper has been immersed in this culture, and I share a real connection on this point. The work that has personally followed me all my life is the most famous manga in the world: One Piece.
