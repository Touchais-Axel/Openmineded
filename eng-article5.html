AI in the creative world

Some time ago in Colorado, Jason Allen won the Space Opera Theatre art competition in the Digital Art category. However, the painting he submitted was actually the creation of an AI. Some say this is a great step forward, others take offense. In this article I will give my opinion.

A masterpiece with a few words

All three of Jason Allen's illustrations, including the one that won the contest, were generated by Midjourney's artificial intelligence, which can create images from scratch with just a few keywords, Vice reports.

Using complex algorithms, these new artificial intelligences have been swarming the Internet for months, offering both ridiculous images and works worthy of great artists. Jason Allen shared his joy and his work on the channel Discord dedicated to Midjourney. A victory that does not please everyone.

A two-way link

Indeed, Internet users are scandalized by this result and are worried about the future of artists with the introduction of these new tools. Others are more malicious and contest Allen's status as an artist. The latter defended himself: "I explored a special commission [...] I created hundreds of images using it, and after many weeks of tweaking and selecting, I chose my three best creations and had them printed on canvas."

"I knew it would cause controversy. It's interesting that all the people on Twitter who are against AI-generated art are the first to forget that there are humans behind the work. Doesn't that seem hypocritical to you?" the artist adds, irritated. Now, everyone has a point of view. Some see AI as an opportunity to discover new facets of art, while others don't see machine creations as such...

Apart from the artistic field, AIs are also gaining importance in medicine. And rightly so, they allow humans to cure diseases that were thought incurable until now. A feat that gives real hope.
