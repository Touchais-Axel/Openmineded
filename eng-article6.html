To heal the impossible

It may have sounded crazy a few years ago, but AI can now help us from a medical perspective. It can, for example, determine whether a patient is likely to be ill. For this, the intelligence doesn't need a deep analysis, hearing the patient's voice is enough. In this article, I explain everything in detail.

The voice, first factor of a long lineage?

It may seem surprising, but our voice says a lot about our character and our mental health. Researchers at an IBM-related laboratory specializing in artificial intelligence are convinced of this.

In the near future, speech analysis will be a powerful tool for interpreting our emotions. To date, a few experienced doctors can clearly and accurately recognize the warning signs of mental illness simply by looking at the way their patients express themselves.

To take this a step further, a team of researchers dedicated to psychiatric research has developed an AI that identifies the verbal traits or tics characteristic of specific mental illnesses. The machine was able to measure the difference in perception between the actual facts and the comments reported by the readers.

An imperfect system

The system, which is still in the testing phase, is available for use by clinicians. Although still in need of improvement, it may be able to positively identify certain vulnerable patients.

Another advance is that the audio analysis does not require the physical presence of the physician at the patient's side. The machine could therefore record the patient's voice and send it to a therapist, who would respond to the request.

Finally, other diseases could potentially be detected with the same technology. Depression, for example, is at the forefront of treatable diseases. Work is also underway to identify Parkinson's, Alzheimer's and other chronic diseases.

Even if dazzling advances are now put on the front of the stage as with IBM and its voice analyzer, advances are still to be expected to one day cure heavier ailments. But let's not lose hope!
